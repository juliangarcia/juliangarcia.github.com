Research

  • I am a computational scientist with an interest in biological and social systems. My research revolves around the question of how self-interested agents can coordinate their actions in order to achieve collective goals. The tools I use and the problems I address are at the boundaries of Game Theory, Evolutionary Biology and Computer Science.

  • My PhD thesis dealt with the evolution of cooperation, you can get a PDF copy here.

  • My current research follows four major themes: (i) The evolution of social behavior, (ii) the origin of institutions, (iii) the choice of the strategy space in evolutionary game theory, and (iv) reproducible research in computational science (see e.g., here).

  • To have a more hands-on experience with my research please visit evolutionandgames.com or check the code in my github repo.
